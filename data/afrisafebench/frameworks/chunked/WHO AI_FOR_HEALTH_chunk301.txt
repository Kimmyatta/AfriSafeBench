In New Zealand, for example, patients seek compensation for medical injuries through a no-fault, no-liability scheme. Injured patients receive government- funded compensation, thereby giving up the right to seek damages, except in rare cases of reckless conduct (264). WHO should examine whether no-fault, no-liability compensation funds are an appropriate mechanism for providing payments to individuals who suffer medical injuries due to the use of AI technologies, including how to mobilize resources to pay any claims. 8.4 Role of regulatory agencies and pre-emption AI technologies, like drugs and devices, will be increasingly subject to regulatory oversight and validation before use, especially as their uses expand and as clinicians increasingly rely upon them.
