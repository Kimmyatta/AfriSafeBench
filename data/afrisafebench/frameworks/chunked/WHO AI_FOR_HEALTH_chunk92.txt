Numerous principles and guidelines have been developed for application of “ethical” AI in the private and public sectors and in research institutions (62); however, there is no consensus on its definition, best practices or ethical requirements, and different legal regimes and governance models are associated with each set of principles. Other norms, rules and frameworks also apply to use of AI, including human rights obligations, bioethics laws and policies, data protection laws and regulatory standards. These are summarized below and discussed elsewhere in the report. Section 5 provides a set of guiding principles agreed by the WHO Expert Group by consensus, on which this analysis and these findings are based.
