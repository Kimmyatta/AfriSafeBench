The inadequacy of the data on people of colour is due to several structural factors, including lack of medical professionals and of adequate information in communities of colour and economic barriers that prevent marginalized communities from seeking health care or participating in research that would allow such individuals to contribute data. Another reason that such machine-learning models are not relevant for people of colour is that developers seek to bring new technologies to the market as quickly as possible. Even if their haste is guided by a desire to reduce avoidable morbidity and mortality, it can replicate existing racial and ethnic disparities, while a more deliberate, inclusive approach to design and development would identify and avoid biased outcomes.
