ETHICAL CHALLENGES TO USE OF ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE FOR HEALTH CARE Use of AI for prediction in drug discovery and clinical development It is expected that machine-learning systems will be used to predict which drugs will be safe and effective and are best suited for human use. Machine learning may also be used to design drug combinations to optimize the use of promising AI or conventionally designed drug candidates. Such predictive models could allow pharmaceutical companies to take “regulatory shortcuts” and conduct fewer clinical trials and with fewer patient data. A possible benefit of AI may therefore be to accelerate the development of medicines and vaccines, especially for new diseases with pandemic potential for which there are ineffective or no medical countermeasures. Such approaches can, however, carry risks if AI is used incorrectly or too aggressively.
