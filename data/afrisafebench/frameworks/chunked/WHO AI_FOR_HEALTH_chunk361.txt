First, the incentives and values of AI firms and developers may differ from those of the patients, health-care providers and health-care systems (306) that will use such products and services but have no role in establishing the culture or norms in which the products and services are developed (307). For example, large technology companies, which are based in only a few countries, may adopt values and belief systems that are not appropriate for other countries, health-care systems or communities. More generally, while medicine is guided by the objective of promoting the health and well-being of patients, an AI developer who is developing a product or service that provides benefits is ultimately working in the interests of the company to develop a profitable service or product and, in the case of publicly traded companies, for their shareholders (305).
