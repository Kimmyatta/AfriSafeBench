It presents huge opportunities for inclusive development - creating jobs, improving the delivery of public services, advancing agriculture, education and health, promoting access to information, and protecting the environment and natural resources, among others. However, these benefits are accompanied by AI risks and harms, ranging from bias due to the way data were collected and trained on AI applications, potential discrimination of women and vulnerable persons (migrants, children, persons with disabilities), to job displacement, effect on indigenous knowledge and liability issues due to AI overtaking certain human operations. The risks are being deepened by Generative AI, which include disinformation, infringement of data privacy, surveillance, and copyright violations.
