• Deal with persons in ways that respect their interests in making decisions about their lives and their person, including health-care decisions, according to informed understanding of the nature of the choice to be made, its significance, the person’s interests and the likely consequences of the alternatives (sometimes called “respect for persons” or “autonomy”). Additional moral requirements can be derived from this list of fundamental moral requirements. For example, safeguarding and protecting individual privacy is not only recognized as a legal requirement in many countries but is also important to enable people to control sensitive information about themselves and self-determination (respect for their autonomy) and to avoid harm.
