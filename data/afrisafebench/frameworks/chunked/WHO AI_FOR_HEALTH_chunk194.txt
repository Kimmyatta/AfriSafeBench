ETHICAL CHALLENGES TO USE OF ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE FOR HEALTH CARE In some cases, automation of routine, mundane functions, such as recording information, could liberate a medical provider to build or enhance a relationship with a patient while AI-guided machines automate certain aspects of caregiving (24). Other mundane functions could be fully assumed by AI, such as automatic adjustment of a hospital ward temperature. The shift to applying AI technologies for more complex areas of clinical care will, however, present several challenges. One is the likely emergence of “peer disagreement” between two competent experts – an AI machine and a doctor (149). In such situations, there is no means of combining the decisions or of reasoning with the algorithm, as it cannot be accessed or engaged to change its mind.
