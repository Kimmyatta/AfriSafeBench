Requiring every clinical AI decision to be “explainable” could also limit the capacity of AI developers to use AI technologies that outperform older systems but which are not explainable (116). Clinical trials provide assurance that unanticipated hazards and consequences of AI- based applications can be identified, addressed and avoided entirely, and additional testing and monitoring of an approved AI device can demonstrate its performance and any changes that may occur after it has been approved. Clinical trials, especially those carried out with diverse populations, can also indicate whether an AI technology is biased against certain sub-groups, races or ethnicities (see below).
