There is also the question of assigning appropriate value to the public’s contribution to development of new AI technologies, such as investment in the development of algorithms, provision of data by individuals and health systems and from health data hubs accessed by private actors for the development of new AI technologies. If AI technologies are increasingly protected by exclusive rights, there is the wider question of whether they will be available, appropriate and affordable in LMIC. Control over and benefit-sharing of big data The central role of big data for AI, including medical big data for use of AI for health care, has led to labelling of data as the new “oil”, a valuable commodity over which there will be increased commercial conflict for its control, use and access (290). Such labelling has been criticized as unhelpful and conceptually inaccurate (291, 292).
