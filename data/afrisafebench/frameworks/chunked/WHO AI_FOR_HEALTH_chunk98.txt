In 2019–2020, the Council established the Ad-hoc Committee on Artificial Intelligence to conduct broad multi-stakeholder consultations in order to determine the feasibility and potential elements of a legal framework for the design and application of AI according to the Council of Europe’s standards on human rights, democracy and the rule of law. Further, in 2019, the Council of Europe released Guidelines on artificial intelligence and data protection (70), also based on the protection of human dignity and safeguarding human rights and fundamental freedom. In addition, the ethical charter of the European Commission for Efficiency of Justice includes five principles relevant to use of AI for health (71). 18 ETHICS AND GOVERNANCE OF ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE FOR HEALTH 4.
