Physicians and nurses will also require a wider range of competence to apply AI in clinical practice, including better understanding of mathematical concepts, the fundamentals of AI, data science, health data provenance, curation, integration and governance (24), and also of the ethical and legal issues associated with the use of AI for health. Such measures (including training) will be necessary to combine and analyse data from many sources adequately, supervise AI tools and detect inaccurate performance of AI (244). Good support and training will ensure that health-care workers and physicians, for example, can avoid common pitfalls such as automation bias when using AI technologies. Eventually, the knowledge, skills and capabilities required of health workers may be defined by professional and statutory regulatory bodies in collaboration with practitioners and educators (24). Significant changes may be made to medical education.
