Community control of health data – data sovereignty and data cooperatives Measures have been taken not only to promote the individual right to privacy and autonomy over health data but also to provide discrete communities with control over their data, including health data, through the exercise of data sovereignty or creation of data cooperatives. Several indigenous communities have sought to establish control over their data through data sovereignty. Māori (the indigenous population of New Zealand) have introduced principles for data sovereignty that establish, for example, control over data, including to protect against future harm, accountability to the people who provide such data by those who collect, use and disseminate them, an obligation for such data to provide a collective benefit, and free prior and informed consent, which, when not obtainable, should be accompanied by stronger governance (276).
