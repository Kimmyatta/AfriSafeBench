ELEMENTS OF A FRAMEWORK FOR GOVERNANCE OF ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE FOR HEALTH (128). A system in which benefits and risks are weighed could, however, invariably lead to sharing of data without consent, as medical benefits – whether better surveillance of disease or development of a new drug – could always be considered more important than a “low risk” of violation of privacy from use of the data. Another concern is that a government or a company may define “public interest” in a way that is not based on public health or patient need.
