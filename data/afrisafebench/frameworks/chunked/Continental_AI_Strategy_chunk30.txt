On the other hand, AI’s impact on the global economy is impressive, according to Price Waterhouse and Coopers (PwC), AI automation has the potential to add $15.7 trillion to the world economy by 2030 and double economic growth rates by 2035 via productivity gains and transformation of how the government and business operate.1 This is equivalent to a 14% gain in global GDP. Businesses and governments have realised AI's potential, and investment in AI has increased manyfold. Global corporate investment in AI increased from 2019 to 2020 by 40% to $67.9 billion. Governments’ spending and focus on AI have also increased. For instance, China's investment in AI is estimated to reach $38.1 billion in 2027, OECD analysis shows that the United States investment in AI increased seventeen-fold between 2001 and 2019, and Europe’s AI Watch data shows investments in the European Union are expected to reach €22.4 billion by 2025.
