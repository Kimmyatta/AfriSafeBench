ETHICAL CHALLENGES TO USE OF ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE FOR HEALTH CARE Fourthly, like all new heath technologies, even if an AI technology does not trigger an ethics warning, its benefits may not be justified by the extra expense or cost (beyond information and communication technology infrastructure) associated with the procurement, training and technology investment required (43). Robotic surgery may produce better outcomes, but the opportunity costs associated with the investment must also be considered. Fifthly, enough consideration may not be given to whether an AI technology is appropriate and adapted to the context of LMIC, such as diverse languages and scripts in a country or among countries (9).
