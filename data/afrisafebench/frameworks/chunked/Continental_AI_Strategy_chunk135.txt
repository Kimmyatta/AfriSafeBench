Africa needs to conduct its own research to establish its own localised position and support innovation to advance solutions that address challenges identified in Agenda 2063 and Suitable development goals. Aspects such as the preservation of local languages, media and culture and how creative industries can be impacted positively and negatively need to be investigated. Thus, AI research and innovation that takes the African context is critical. Research is also needed to understand the ethical and legal, safety and security implications of AI on African people. 48 Research and innovation require the presence of highly trained and well-equipped researchers who dedicate their time and energy to investigating the different facets of AI and are open to collaborating globally. Researchers need centres of excellence that are equipped with state-of-the-art tools (GPUs, Cloud, HPC, etc.) and access to data sets to train algorithms.
