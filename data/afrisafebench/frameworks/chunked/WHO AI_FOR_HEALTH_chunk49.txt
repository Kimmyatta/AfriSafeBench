People have always been at the centre at all levels of decision-making in health care, whereas the inevitable growth of AI for health care could eventually challenge human primacy over medicine and health. This guidance is also designed for those responsible for the design, deployment and refinement of AI technologies, including technologists and software developers. Finally, it is intended to guide the companies, universities, medical associations and international organizations that will, with governments and ministries of health, set policies and practices to define use of AI in the health sector. In identifying the many ethical concerns raised by AI and by providing the relevant ethical frameworks to address such concerns, this document is intended to support responsible use of AI worldwide.
