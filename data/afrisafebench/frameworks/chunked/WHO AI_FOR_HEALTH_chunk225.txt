Box 7. AI technologies for detecting skin cancer exclude people of colour. Machine learning has outperformed dermatologists in detecting potentially cancerous skin lesions. As rates of skin cancer increase in many countries, AI technology would improve the ability of dermatologists to diagnose skin cancer. The data used to train one highly accurate machine-learning model are, however, for “fair-skinned” populations in Australia, Europe and the USA. Thus, while the technology assists in diagnosis, prevention and treatment of skin cancer in white and light-skinned individuals, the algorithm was neither appropriate nor relevant for people of colour, as it was not trained on images of these populations.
