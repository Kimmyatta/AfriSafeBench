Whether patients share the benefits may depend on the entity with which they are shared, such as commercial actors, which may not share benefits if the medical products and services are neither affordable nor available (see below). Thus, conscripting health data with the broad goal of contributing to the public good is questionable when the data are shared with a commercial entity, whatever the intended product or service. Recent instances (described in Section 6.3) of patient data that were shared by not-for-profit entities or academic institutions with private companies without the consent of the patients have raised significant concern, as the patients were not notified that their data were shared, for what purpose or the identity of the private entity.
