The emergence of digital identification in the COVID-19 pandemic The COVID-19 pandemic is expanding and accelerating the creation of infrastructure for digital identities to store health data for several uses. In China, a QR code system has been established from the digital payment system established by Alipay, a mobile and online payment platform, to introduce an “Alipay Health Code”, in which the data collected are used to establish an algorithm to “draw automated conclusions as to whether someone is a contagion risk” (119). For a national programme to vaccinate millions of people against SARS-Cov2, India may use its national digital ID system, Aadhar, to avoid duplication and to track beneficiaries (120).
