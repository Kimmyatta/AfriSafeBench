1. WHO shall treat data as a public good. 2. WHO shall uphold Member States’ trust in data. 3. WHO shall support Member States’ data and health information systems capacity. 4. WHO shall be a responsible data manager and steward. 5. WHO shall strive to fill public health data gaps. WHO is also introducing a data governance framework that would introduce the necessary standards, solutions and structures to ensure the quality and integrity of WHO data, from collection, storage, analysis and validation through to use. To ensure that the principles can be put into practice, WHO will use a “hub-and-spoke” governance model to obtain feedback and approval, and data focal points at WHO will work with regional focal points on issues that arise during the ever-growing use of health data. They will also be guided by the Data Governance Committee constituted by WHO (282).
