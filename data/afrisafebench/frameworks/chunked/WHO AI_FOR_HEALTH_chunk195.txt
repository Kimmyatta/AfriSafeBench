There are also no clear rules for determining who is right, and if a patient is left to trust either a technology or a physician, the decision may depend on factors that have no basis in the “expertise” of the machine or the doctor. Choosing one of the two leads to an undesirable outcome. If the doctor ignores the machine, AI has added little value (149). If the doctor accepts the machine’s decision, it may undermine his or her authority and weaken their accountability. Some may argue that the recommendation of an algorithm should be preferred, as it combines the expertise of multiple experts and many data points (149). The challenge of human–computer interactions has been addressed by validating systems, providing appropriate education for users and validating the systems continuously. It may, however, be ethically challenging for doctors to rely on the judgement of AI, as they have to accept decisions based on black-box algorithms (159).
