BUILDING AN ETHICAL APPROACH TO USE OF ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE FOR HEALTH Putting prediction to good use Use of AI for prognosis will allow assessment of the relative risk of disease and predict illness. There are, however, several risks and challenges with the use of predictive analytics, including concern about the accuracy of the predictions and that prediction of a negative outcome could affect an individual’s autonomy and well-being. In public health, predictive analytics can forecast major health events, including outbreaks, before they occur. For example, before the COVID-19 pandemic, WHO was developing EPI-BRAIN, a global platform that will allow experts in data and public health to analyse large datasets for use in emergency preparedness and response (234). It would allow forecasting and early detection of threats of infection and their impact on the basis of scenarios, simulation exercises and insights to improve coordinated decision-making and response.
