As noted above, technology companies have begun to issue guiding principles for the use of AI; however, they are sometimes viewed as “ethics washing”, may create a gap in responsibility (assigning responsibility for retrospective harm), do not involve the public in their development and may be administered in a way that is not transparent to the public or to governments, with no involvement of the public or an independent authority for oversight of adherence to the principles. Provision of health care by the private sector outside the health-care system The proliferation of AI applications for health outside the health-care system may extend access to some health-care advice; however, such applications raise new questions and concerns.
