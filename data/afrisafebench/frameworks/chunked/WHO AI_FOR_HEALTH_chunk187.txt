ETHICAL CHALLENGES TO USE OF ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE FOR HEALTH CARE AI provides great power and benefits (including the possibility of profit) to those who design and deploy such systems. Thus, reciprocity should apply – companies that reap direct and indirect benefits from AI-guided technologies should also have to shoulder responsibility for any negative consequences (section 8), especially as it is health- care providers who will bear the immediate brunt of any psychological stress if an AI technology causes harm to a patient. Companies should also allow independent audits and oversight of enforcement of its own ethics standards to ensure that the standards are being met and that corrective action is taken if a problem arises. Accountability for AI-related errors and harm Clinicians already use many non-AI technologies in diagnosis and treatment, such as X-rays and computer software.
