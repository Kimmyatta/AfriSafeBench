Data cooperatives allow members to set common ethical standards, and some have developed their own tools and applications to ensure that the data are used beneficially (113). Federated data Federated data systems have grown significantly. They include collaborations between research institutions, governments and the public and private sector and within the private sector. Federated data-sharing has been defined as “a promising way to enable access to health data, including genomic data, that must remain inside a country or institution because of their sensitivity” (278). Data do not leave the participating organization that holds them, but authorized users can make queries that allow them to access data, for example to train an algorithm.
